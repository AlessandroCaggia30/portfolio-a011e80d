About

I am a final year MSc student in Economics and Social Sciences at Bocconi University. My research interests lie in applied micro, labor economics, and economics of institutions and culture.

Currently I am a research intern at Harvard University with Prof. Awa Ambra Seck, working with historical microdata aiming to build a structured longitudinal database. Previously, I worked as an RA at the Dondena Center (Bocconi) and with Prof. Massimo Anelli.

My main working paper studies the labor market effects of Italy's 2016 anti-labor-exploitation reform using a triple difference-in-differences strategy with a Bartik instrument.
